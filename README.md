# 📌 Project Overview
This project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Netflix dataset to understand the platform's content landscape. By analyzing over 8,800 titles, we identify what Netflix prioritizes, where their content comes from, and where the potential "gaps" in their library exist.


# 📊 Key Business Insights
1.Through data cleaning and visualization, the following seven key trends were identified:

2.Movies are King: Nearly 70% of the platform consists of Movies, while TV Shows make up only 30%.

3.The "Mature" Focus: The library is heavily geared toward adults and teens, with TV-MA and TV-14 being the most common ratings.

4.Global Leaders: The United States and India remain the primary content providers for the platform.

5.The 2019 Peak: Content additions reached an all-time high in 2019 before experiencing a slight post-pandemic stabilization.

6.New Over Old: Netflix focuses on modern hits; the vast majority of their library was released after 2010.

7.The 100-Minute Sweet Spot: Most movies on the platform follow a standard format of approximately 100 minutes.

8.Fresh TV Content: Unlike their movie library, which contains some older titles, their TV Show selection is almost entirely recent.


# 🛠️ Data Process
Cleaning: Handled missing values in director, cast, and country to ensure accurate reporting.

Standardization: Converted dates to a unified format and stripped unnecessary whitespace from categories.

Visualization: Used Python libraries to create clear charts showing content growth and geographic distribution.

# 💡 Strategic Recommendations
Based on the data, the following steps are recommended to drive subscriber growth:

Expand Kids & Family Content: This is currently a smaller segment of the library compared to mature ratings.

Balance the Ratio: Increasing the volume of TV Shows could help compete with platforms that focus heavily on episodic engagement.
